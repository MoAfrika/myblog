Javascript and I

It wasn’t < LOVE > at first <FUNCTION>, but after a thousand <ERRORS>, we’re finally in <SYNC>

"From Infinite Loops to Tiny Wins:
My Love-Hate Relationship with JavaScript"


Starting with JavaScript felt like stepping into an unpredictable puzzle where every piece promised to fit, but not without a little struggle. It wasn’t about an instant mastery of loops, promises, or hoisting, but more about understanding the rules of this chaotic world. It’s not about complaining or feeling defeated—no, it's about embracing the challenge, understanding it, and finding ways to make it work for you.

When I first got my hands on JavaScript, it felt like I was trying to crack a code... within a code. On the surface, JavaScript seems like a beast with a thousand heads .... functions, arrays, objects, you name it. And when you add concepts like asynchronous behavior and hoisting to the mix, well, let’s just say I had my work cut out for me.
One of the first things that tripped me up was loops. They seemed simple enough, right? Loop through an array or repeat some logic, and voilà, you’re golden. But, turns out, loops in JavaScript don’t just work by repeating .... they work on their own terms. Whether it's a for loop, while loop, or do-while loop, they play by their own rhythm, and you, my friend, are the one who has to learn the dance moves. It wasn’t until I started actually understanding how loops function in JavaScript that I began to get the hang of it. The key was in the details—the conditions, the counters, and how small shifts in logic could make all the difference.
Then, there’s asynchronous code. If you've ever used a service that makes you wait .... like waiting for your Uber or waiting for someone to text you back,you’ll get what Promises are about. Promises in JavaScript let you handle operations that take time, without holding up the rest of the program.
But when I first tried working with promises, they felt like those friends who promise to show up but never do. You write a function expecting a result, and instead, you’re stuck waiting for something that’s still "pending." Async functions stepped in like a cool friend who finally explained how the system works: “Don’t worry, everything will resolve, but give it time.”
The beauty of JavaScript’s event loop is in how it allows us to manage tasks without blocking the thread. The idea is that while one part of your code is waiting on a response (say, fetching data from an API), the rest of the code keeps running. In simpler terms: your program doesn’t sit and twiddle its thumbs while it waits. It keeps moving, keeps processing. The challenge for me was not just understanding that, but when things actually happen in the event loop—how operations are queued and processed based on priority.

What was the most important lesson I learned during all this? Consistency. It’s not about getting everything right the first time or understanding every concept on the first try—it’s about showing up. There are times when it feels like you’re just throwing code at a wall to see if it sticks, but the more you write, the clearer things get. Each line of code brings you one step closer to mastering the fundamentals. And when you grasp those, you build a foundation strong enough to tackle even the more complex libraries and frameworks out there.

But it wasn’t just about the code itself .... it was about managing life while learning code. Balancing family, kids, and a thousand distractions while trying to focus on learning JavaScript meant that, sometimes, I had to shut everything else out and focus on the basics. Learning the language took time, and I had to accept that. I needed to be patient with myself. So many distractions, pressures, and self-doubt nearly got in the way of progress, but the more I stuck with it, the more I realized: this was not just about learning JavaScript—it was about proving to myself that I could push through even when it felt like I was getting nowhere.

And now, two years later, I’m not just working through frameworks or libraries; I’m putting together my portfolio .... something I didn’t think would ever be possible when I first started. The road was winding, yes, but the more I understood the core concepts of JavaScript, the more I could move forward. Was it easy? Hell no. Was it worth it? Absolutely.

The moral of this story is simple: JavaScript is a challenge, but it’s also a tool. Once you learn its ins and outs, you’ll find that it’s not the scary monster it once seemed.
The hardest part is showing up each day, even when you’re not sure where the code will take you. But if you do, you’ll make progress—and that’s something you can’t put a price on.
So, to anyone just starting this journey: stay consistent, focus on the basics, and know that every bug, every frustration, and every moment of confusion is part of the process. The reward is in the mastery you’ll gain along the way.
