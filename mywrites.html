How I Finally Understood Recursion (and My Family Tree)

What do JavaScript functions and family reunions have in common? More than you think. A funny, mind-bending journey into recursive logic—and family drama.

Picture this: You’re at a family reunion. Auntie calls Uncle who calls Cousin who calls Auntie again... and suddenly you're stuck in a loop of greetings and “where have you beens?” Welcome to recursion.

Recursion used to fry my brain. A function that calls itself? Why? Just loop it, right? But then I realized—recursion is just a way to break down a big problem into smaller versions of itself. Like how tracing my family tree made me realize I’m just a reused function with different arguments.

The base case? That one ancestor everyone says “was wild back in the day.” The recursive case? Every new generation born into the same drama loop, just with slightly updated variables (read: family WhatsApp groups and midlife crises).

The magic clicked when I wrote a function to flatten nested arrays. It was just like navigating extended family. You dive deep, peel back each layer, and at the end of it, you’ve got one clean array (or at least a decent family photo). Now I use recursion for all sorts of things: tree structures, algorithms, and explaining life crises.

My tip? Start with the base case. Always. Forget that, and you'll get stuck deeper than a cousin explaining their crypto startup.
