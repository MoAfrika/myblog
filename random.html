RAW THOUGHTS, NO CHASER

Stray musings, sharp truths, and the occasional existential Molotov.

Build Yourself Into a Legend (And Let the Rest Regret Their Role)

Not every door is closed because of bad luck. Some doors are closed because the person you are today isn't the one who deserves to walk through them yet. That's the part they don't write in success stories.

You can't fake readiness. You can’t tweet your way into evolution. Growth doesn’t care about your intentions. It only answers to transformation. Real, uncomfortable, long-haul transformation.

I know because I’ve lived through it — the burn of betrayal, the silence of fake friends, the weight of starting over with nothing but ambition and spite.

I Don’t Do Second Chances Anymore

I used to be forgiving. Maybe too forgiving. I used to believe in reconciliation, in “talking it out,” in “they didn’t mean it.”

Now? I believe in receipts. In red flags. In cutting the cord and keeping it cut. It’s not easy to get on my wrong side, but once you do... get comfortable. You might be staying a while.

I’ve learned to love from a distance. To mourn people while they’re still alive. It sounds cold, but it’s survival. It's knowing that peace comes with boundaries, and boundaries come with grief.

There’s Power in Walking Alone

There’s a version of you that only emerges in solitude. The legendary version. The one that doesn’t need validation, doesn’t chase apologies, doesn’t make space for fair-weather fans.

You become a legend in the shadows. You sharpen your vision in silence. You outgrow explanations, and you let your glow-up speak fluent revenge.

Because here's the truth — those who mistreated you don’t get front row seats to your evolution. They don't get to toast your wins or fake-laugh at your success stories.

The Facade of Family (Just a Touch)

Blood is thicker than water, sure... but so is oil, and try mixing that with your peace. Sometimes, the ones you were raised around are the very ones who can’t stand to see you rise.

Don’t beg to be understood in rooms where you were only meant to outgrow the furniture. Let them gossip. Let them guess. Let them grieve your absence in their group chats.

Be Loud With Your Becoming

Document it. Share it. Don’t wait until you’ve “made it” to show up online. Talk while it’s still messy. Share while it still hurts. Let the real ones find you through the noise.

Your people will know. Your people will come. But only when you stop hiding the process to protect the feelings of those who never protected yours.

Build yourself like your future is watching. Because it is.

Mayday: The Day My Mother Died (And I Woke the Hell Up)

24 May. A day the world did forget, but I never will.

They say that time heals. Time, and maybe Rooibos tea. Lies. Time just teaches you how to wear the pain like a frayed old hoodie. Still bloody. Still frayed. But it fits now.

It's one year. One year since my mum died. One year since cancer cleared the ground away from under my already precarious existence. One year since the woman who raised me, corrected me, released me into the wild, stopped breathing.

And lo and behold, twelve months later. World's still turning. Electricity still goes out. People still exchange food they didn't cook. And I? I'm launching a legitimate online store.

But let's be real...

If My Mom Was Still Alive, I Wouldn't Own a Store.

Let that sink in.

I wouldn’t be this obsessed, this focused, this unhinged from comfort. I would’ve never watched 600 hours of UI tutorials. Never rebuilt my identity from burnt-out code and burnt-out dreams.

But grief, grief is a boot to the chest.

You either stay down or you get the hell up and make noise.

I stood up, noisily...

How Long Is Mourning Supposed to Last?

A week? A month? Until the uncles stop asking if you've "moved on"?

Grief doesn't get a time sheet. It just clocks in whenever it wants to. 2AM. 5PM. In the Pick n Pay aisle next to the cans of Lucky Star that remind you of Sunday pap.

Grief is a monster like that.

There are days I laugh and work 18 hours straight, like a crazed man...

Then there are nights I cry into a keyboard as though I'm attempting to water the keys...

And then there are days I simply sit. Staring at nothing. Pondering everything...

Mourning doesn't require permission. It simply barrels in like an obnoxious cousin and consumes all your peace.

This Past Year? The Ghetto.

Emotionally? Spiritually? Financially?

Broke. Broken. Yet also... becoming.

Ever asked for food and they spoke to you like you asked for their liver?

Ever borrowed cash and got blueticked faster than your own thoughts?

Ever cried "hungry" and the fridge creaked louder than you?

Ever slept at someone's place knowing your presence caused the walls discomfort?

That year gave that. All of that. No filter. No sugarcoating.

And guess what? I'm grateful.

No seriously. I love the struggle. Not because I idealize it, but because it puts things into perspective...

People. Yourself. The family that only shows up in funeral pictures. The ones who pray for you louder than they ever supported you. The ones who "miss her" but can't even get your name right.

I've Never Been Poor. But I've Been Financially Disrespected.

Being poor is one thing. But that spiritual poverty, when people bring you to your lowest hour and deem it adorable fodder for their next brunch chatter?

Whew. That is poverty.

I've gone for walks aware the joke was on me...

I've sat with others who laughed as I spoke and snapped my travails for amusement...

I've constructed on borrowed Wi-Fi, prayed for data packages, and worked harder than sin.

I am not ashamed. I'm hard-worn.

So Why a Store?

Because this is not a store. It's a shrine. A community. A spiritual revolt...

It's where mamas live on, through stories, through stitches, through the love they left behind...

It's where papas speak, really speak. Not just soccer and Spotify, but what it means to raise children when you're still healing the child in you...

Is that not worth celebrating?

It’s where people who’ve ever lost something, a person, a dream, a version of themselves, come to feel seen.

And it’s built by someone who was never supposed to make it...

But did. Is. Will.

Final Tears. Final Words.

This article? My last cry. Not because I’m done mourning, but because I’ve learned how to carry her with me now...

She is the code. The caption. The copy. The customer experience...

She is the WHY of the all-nighters, the design tweaks, the constant marketing hustle.

She did not die for me to fold.

So here's to you, Mama...

Mayday will always hurt. But from now on, it'll also spark legacy.

And to everyone reading this: if you're still grieving, still struggling, still picking up the pieces...

You are not alone. But you are not done.

The Great Cosmic Giggle & My Brain's Bizarre Upgrade...Is it one?

Hear me, I've never been one to shy away from raising an eyebrow at anything that even slightly smells of "miracle" or "divine intervention." My default position on anything supernatural is skeptical optimism — heavy on the skepticism.

So imagine my surprise, and quite frankly, my utter flabbergastation, when things started getting weird... and not "you-find-a-chicken-bone-in-your-shoe" weird. More like "the-ancestors-are-calling-but-I-don't-have-airtime" weird.

Moses, The Burning Bush, & My Sudden Interest in... Interest?

And then there's this. This Moses business. Now, come on, Moses. The staff dude and the water-parting trick. Always seemed a bit of a Sunday school blockbuster to me. But lately, I relate more than I'm comfortable admitting.

Think about it. This man, Moses, he's just going about his sheep-herding business, complaining probably about the drought and the price of lamb, when he sees this bush that's burning but not burning out.

But Moses? He gets interested. He gets curious. He doesn't swipe past it like another TikTok video. He turns to look. And that, my friend, is where it gets freaky. Because it means the universe actually responds to the curious. The ones who slow down and ask why.

And this, this is the part that's gnawing at me like a persistent township dog. It's whispering something unsettling... "What excites you, what really makes you curious, is where your calling is hiding."

It's scary, really, because it means every stray thought, every deep-dive into a Wikipedia wormhole, every passing fascination could actually be... God. Or something equally invested in your growth.

Jesus, Daniel, & The Freaky Feeling of Remembering

And it's not just Moses. It's Jesus, the ultimate disruptor, flipping tables and expectations. It's Daniel, chilling with lions like they're oversized kittens. These stories are weird, but they're also strangely familiar.

And that's the part that seriously freaks me out. It's like finding old photographs in an attic you didn’t even know existed... and realizing they're photos of your family, your people, your blood.

It's unsettling. It's profound. And frankly, it’s a bit hilarious in its sheer absurdity. Am I finally tapping into some ancestral spiritual download, or am I coming down with a bad case of the existential giggles?

But this feeling, this remembering...it’s insistent. It’s a pull that’s more powerful than logic, more relentless than doubt. And it scares the living daylights out of me — in the best way.

How are you walking the sacred whispers in your own life?

The Great South African Man-Drought: Are We Still Here, Or Just Taking Up Space?

Let's call a spade a spade, our townships, from windblown Khayelitsha flats to Thembisa's sprawling concrete jungle, are experiencing a bad dose of "Leadership Vapour-Lock" — a man-drought of epic proportions.

Need a hair-catching laugh? Drive through any respectable kasi. You'll see him, Bra Joe, or Mlungisi, or Young Sizwe, a rugby prop-sized man, muscles bursting out of... well, not much else but frustration and Lotto tickets.

The WiFi Zoo, The Soapie Sages & The Silence of Real Life

Then you come to town, or even just the taxi rank locally with a router that works. It's a moment from a satirical documentary. Our men, clustered together like stunned, green-eyed wildebeest, sharing TikTok videos and "deep" WhatsApp memes.

Then, however, magic occurs. You say something about the latest twist in Uzalo or Skeem Saam. Or the latest drama between Messi and Ronaldo. Or what celebrity is now "cancelling" the other. Suddenly, everyone is an expert — but only in fiction.

But stand them on their heads and ask them to dissect the systemic ills that suffocate our communities, the non-existent opportunities, the absent fathers, the violence spiralling like load shedding — and the silence is as wide as the Vaal.

The Unseen Calling: Why I'm Stepping Out

I've spent a lifetime avoiding the limelight. If I did my music, it was not to be noticed, about the flash, the ego trip. I wanted to be heard, not watched. The rhythm, the message, the echo in the soul.

But now... something's different. There's a pull, a rumble, low and steady, from the earth itself that I stand on. It's not a whisper now, it's a growl that shakes my bones.

The Siren Song of the Shaky Ground

Let’s get brutally frank. There’s a little energy in the nightlife here, isn’t there? Especially with our “Groovists and DJs.” These aren’t just people... they’re architects of the township’s heartbeat.

I’ve seen it more times than I care to recall. A bright, young spark, full of dreams, with a beat in their heart and hopes as broad as the Carletonville sky. They play around, they dabble... until the dabble turns to dependence.

The One-Way Ticket to "Oops"

Here’s the unvarnished reality, fresh from the school of hard knocks: that “dabbling” phase? It’s briefer than a politician’s promise. The playful splash turns into a tidal wave, the party into a pit. Suddenly, you’re not dancing — you’re drowning.

For some, the escape becomes a habit. The high turns into a hole. It’s like building a card house on a fault line... okay for a bit, until it all collapses. And when it does, the pieces cut deep.

The Exit Strategy: A Tight Squeeze

That line you whispered... “the way out is so narrow”? You weren’t kidding. It’s like shoving a camel through a needle’s eye after a bender. When someone’s caught in the groove, breaking out is a dance with the devil.

Imagine walking through a dark, twisting tunnel with no flashlight and no map, while everyone else strolls down wide, sunny boulevards. That’s what it feels like. And the tunnel is lined with echoes of every bad decision you ever made.

The Pulse of Merafong, and Its Fever

You see these kids. Rhythm in their bones. DJs and Groovists trying to build joy from broken dreams. They are the heartbeat of Merafong, pounding even as the mines cave and the streetlights flicker out.

But you’ve also seen the edge of the beat, where the lights don’t reach. The moment a high note becomes a downfall. Where escape morphs into exile. The party dies. The music stops. Addiction steals the future note by note.

I Am the Mirror

I am not just a voice. I am the Mirror of Merafong. My hometown. If you see me, you see the streets.

This is not some textbook parable. This is our lived, bleeding truth. Etched in every twitch of a dancer’s smile, every corner where laughter hides desperation. We must see ourselves, bruises and all, before we can heal.

Because the music might stop. But the echoes don’t have to be silent.

The Great Word Limit: A Post From the Other Side of Thirty-Something (and Diapers)

Come on, friend. In hindsight, from this place on the verge of 40, with a couple little ones surrounding me who require my every moment and word, I can promise you this: we do not have unlimited words for each other. Not in a day. Not in a lifetime.

But what if, just imagine, one day we woke up and found out there was a word limit... 1000 words. That's your daily allowance, no top-ups, no dipping into tomorrow. A virtual data cap for your mouth.

The Great Purge of Pointless Prose

And first and foremost, all that wordy rubbish? Gone. Like trying to get a solid internet connection when there's a hurricane above. No more dry summaries of soapie storylines. No more “just checking in” at the start of every WhatsApp chat. Only the meat, none of the garnish.

Meetings would transform from rambling, multi-hour sagas that make you yearn to chew your arm away, into tight, mean, laser-like conversation. All of them, keeping their word count like calories before a wedding.

Relationships would also get a solid shake-down. Arguments would be stripped bare, without over-the-top speeches or the passive-aggressive tango's we've perfected. Each accusation tossed like a grenade, each apology rationed as if it cost airtime.

The Unintended Consequences (Because There Always Are)

There would not be all sunshine and roses, of course? Not everything would be lekker. The world would become a minefield of unspoken annoyances. Like trying to explain the new SASSA rules to your gogo in five words or less. Therapists? Out of work. Replaced by silent, sympathetic nods and maybe a mental, "Eish, I feel you." Misunderstandings? They'd skyrocket. Nuance, that delicate butterfly of real dialogue, would vanish into thin air.

And don't forget the strategy word-hoarders. Those cunning individuals who judiciously save up their words so that they can have maximum effect, unloading a bucket of eloquence when everyone else is speechless at day's end.

The Silent Revolution

Ultimately, a thousand-word per day restriction wouldn't just change how we speak; it would radically shift what exactly we treasure in talk. From this viewpoint, with the wisdom of almost-40 and a midnight nappy run ahead of me, I say: let’s use what we have wisely. My bet? A little of each, like a well-balanced flavor profile... a dash of this, a dash of that. But one thing is for sure: all that endless, senseless talking would finally be over.

The Sonic Wallpaper of My Skull

Since the start, I've enjoyed "the wrong ear" that my cousins used to call mine. While they were out there dancing to whatever Metro FM was serving, or yelling boy band choruses, I was floating in my own sonic universe.

I wasn't supposed to hear that. That's the catch, music, to me, was never a case of "supposed to." It was a case of honesty. Not the buffed, sterilized, chart-friendly kind. The kind you have to dig for. The kind that sits in your chest and rattles your bones.

I chose the underground ones because they spoke to me. I wasn't chart-in-love. That wasn't my life. I ached for the late-night confessionals, the rage diary-writing turned into songs, the truth crammed into three minutes of static and soul.

And I didn’t just carry global chaos within me, I also brought my South African soul with me. Brenda Fassie on Sunday mornings. Zola when I needed to toughen up. Tumi when I needed to think. ProKid on the days I needed to dream.

No one really understood. "Why don't you just make a normal playlist?" they'd say. But to me, that was like asking someone to write a love note in Arial. No way, man. My playlist was my fingerprint, my mood board, my medicine.

For me, music wasn’t just music. It was medicine. Mirror. Rebellion and comfort at the same time. It wasn't just noise, it was proof I wasn't alone. It was the audio wallpaper of my skull.

Then one time, everything changed. I started to hear songs in my own head. Lyrics stuck to my lips like lines waiting to be written. I quit looking for songs that told my story and started making my own noise, my own truth. That is how the listener became the artist.

Two Altars, One Truth!

I have stood in the smoke of burning incense, watching blood darken the soil as elders murmured the names of those who came before me. I have lifted a calabash to my lips, felt tradition humming in my veins as prayers for protection mingled with the cries of goats and the laughter of children.

And then, just as the fire cracked and the meat sizzled, someone started a hymn. “There is power in the blood of the Lamb!” The same hands that held the blade to a goat’s neck lifted in worship, voices trembling with memory and hope.

I grew up here. Between two worlds. Between libations poured for those beneath the soil and prayers whispered to the man who walked on water. Between bones thrown in the dust and bread broken at the altar.

What are we? A people torn between who we were and who we were forced to become. A people who fear turning our backs on the old ways but also fear hell for not embracing the new. We are both. We are neither.

And maybe that’s why, no matter how loud we sing, neither God nor our ancestors answer us the way we wish they would.

The Lesson

I’m not angry… not at you, not at fate, not at the knife you slid between my ribs, slow, deliberate, like a lover’s hand tracing the spine of a dying romance. You only taught me what I needed to know, that trust is a borrowed cigarette, lit in the wind, smoked too fast, burning out before warmth ever settles. I should’ve known better… now I do. Your betrayal wasn’t a wound, just a lesson written in blood, signed with a smirk… and silence. No hard feelings… just a closed door.

Faithful, but Failing

You don’t cheat, congratulations. You kept your hands to yourself, but did you keep your heart open? You stayed, but did you show up? You were present, but did you participate? Did you give, or just hang around for the credit?

Love is not a courtroom where fidelity is your only defense. You think not stepping out makes you step up? No. You can be faithful and still be a storm your partner has to survive.

Because what is loyalty if your love is lazy… if your words cut… if your silence drowns… if your presence feels like absence? What is faith if the only thing you give is your body, but never your spirit? What is partnership if you’re always tired, always distracted, always somewhere else?

So, I ask again: what is loyalty if it’s not love in action? What is it really worth?
