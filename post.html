How I Finally Understood Recursion (and My Family Tree)

⚡ How I Finally Understood Recursion (and My Family Tree)

"It took tracing my family’s history to finally understand that recursion isn’t just a function calling itself — it’s a story passed down, one generation at a time."

by MoAfrika Skotch Ntilane • April 12, 2025

For the longest time, I couldn't wrap my head around recursion. It felt like one of those concepts that was just... too abstract. You know, when you're told that a function calls itself, but it doesn't click? I was stuck in that loop for years.

It wasn’t until I started researching my family history that it finally made sense. Recursion, in its essence, isn’t just a mathematical concept or a programming trick — it’s a story of repetition, of the same pattern coming back around.

Here’s how it works: Think about your family tree. Your family is like a recursive function. The first generation (your grandparents) is passed down to the next generation (your parents), and then passed down again to you. Each generation is its own call, passing on a little more of the story, a little more of the code that defines the next one.

Just like in recursion, the base case is the starting point. It’s the first ancestor you trace, the first function call that defines the rest of the sequence. As you go deeper, you see that each layer builds on the last — sometimes with small tweaks, sometimes with major shifts. But at the core, the same structure repeats. The recursion works because each call brings you closer to the answer — or in my case, to the understanding that everything connects back.

Recursion is a function of memory, of layers, and of time. And when I connected it with the story of my ancestors, it clicked in a way that textbooks never could.
